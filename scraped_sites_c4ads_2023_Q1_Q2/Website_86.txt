Representative Image KORAPUT: A joint team of forest officials of Odisha and Chhattisgarh raided Kalamdangari village in Nabarangpur district and seized a leopard skin on Friday. Three persons were arrested in this connection. The accused have been identified as Kush Bhatra, Damuru Majhi and Mohan Singh Raut, forest officials said."We had specific information that a leopard skin was kept in a house in Kalamdangari village.As the area borders Chhattisgarh, there was a chance that the accused may flee to the neighbouring state. So we took the help of our Indgaon counterparts in Chhattisgarh," said Tanuja Kartik, forest range officer, Raighar. "The leopard skin, sharp-edged weapons, bow and arrow, and the carcasses of a few more wild animals were seized from the residence of Majhi. We raided at least 15 houses in Kalamdangari," an official said.
