The pangolin scales were being transported from Aizawl to Zokhawthar AIZAWL: Assam Rifles seized 44kg of pangolin scales on the Mizoram-Myanmar border near Melbuk on Saturday.The pangolin scales were being transported from Aizawl to Zokhawthar.A press statement issued by the paramilitary force said the seized contraband has a market value of Rs 44 lakh.Two persons - Lalmuankima (39) and Isaac (38) - both residents of Aizawl, were arrested for alleged smuggling of pangolin scales and handed over to the customs officials at the land customs station in Zokhawthar along with the seized contraband.Enforcement agencies, including the state police and central organisations, had seized large quantities of pangolin scales while being clandestinely smuggled to China via Myanmar.Meanwhile, the Aizawl battalion of Assam Rifles and 23 Sector headquarters organised a cleanliness drive of river Chite Lui as part of World Environment Day major outreach activities for mass mobilisation on Mission LiFE (Lifestyle for Environment) by the Assam Rifles troops along with locals and civil society organisations."The objective was to spread awareness about the importance and role of rivers in our day-to-day life and environment as a whole," the Assam Rifles said in a statement.River Chite flows along the east and southern side of the capital Aizawl city.Explore Your Financial Landscape
