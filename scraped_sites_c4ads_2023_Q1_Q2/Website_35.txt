The team found eight slings, two axes and scales of three pangolins hidden inside the vehicle, which was carrying 13 people, except the driver.All the 13 were arrested and the weapons and the scales of pangolins were seized.” “Pangolins are protected under Schedule 1 of the Wildlife (Protection) Act 1972 that prohibits their hunting, trade or any other form of utilization,” he said adding that during interrogation led by SDO Anil Vishwakarma, the accused revealed that they were cutting trees in forest illegally near Santoshi Mata Mandir in Ghagharala area for the past four days.“The accused also revealed that they killed deer, boar, wild rabbit and pangolin, fried and ate them during the four days,” the DFO said. A case under section 9, 39, 48A, 50 and 51 of the Wildlife (Protection) Act 1972 that can result in seven years of imprisonment and heavy penalty was registered against the accused, he added. “We have also seized the four-wheeler used for transporting the accused, while the driver is being questioned for his involvement in the crime,” he said.“In a separate action, forest department has caught Anarsingh, one of the prime accused of cutting trees in around 5 hectare area of Nepanagar forest range,” the DFO said adding that the forest department has recently started rewarding cash prize of Rs 1000 to Rs 5000 to informers for giving right and valuable information regarding encroachment, wildlife hunting, mining and other illegal activities in jungles.Explore Your Financial Landscape with
